Filming Guide

Before You Start - Read Once

iPhone Settings

Resolution4K at 30fps
FormatHigh Efficiency
StabilizationStandard - ON
White Balance LockON
OrientationVertical - always
Tap your face to lock focus before every video. Hold until AE/AF LOCK appears at the top.

Framing

  • Eyes in the top third of the frame - not centered
  • Small space above your head - two fingers worth
  • Shoulders and chest in frame always
  • Solo: 3-4 feet from the phone
  • Duo: both 4-5 feet back, side by side, equal space
  • Phone at eye level or very slightly above

Audio

  • DJI Mic Mini clipped 6-8 inches below your chin
  • Connect to iPhone via USB-C before opening camera
  • 10-second test recording before the first video
  • Close doors, turn off anything that hums

Delivery - New This Session

  • Hooks are written to be delivered straight to camera - deadpan, confident, no preamble
  • Say the hook exactly as written then make it your own from there
  • 20 second reels - get in, say the thing, get out. No filler.
  • If you nail a take, send that one. If unsure, send all takes.
The hook is the most important line in every video. Don't rush it and don't soften it - let it land before you move on.
Closing Disclaimer - Pick One Per Video

Every video closes with a disclaimer. Keep it natural - deliver it like you would any other line, not like a legal read. Pick whichever one feels right for the video you just filmed. All three work.

Option 1 - Recommended

"Not advice, just perspective - your situation is unique, let's talk about it."

Option 2

"This is our take, not personalized advice - reach out if you want to dig into your specific situation."

Option 3

"Everything we share is our opinion - for advice specific to you, let's have a real conversation."

For shorter 20 second reels, Option 1 flows best. For longer duo videos, Option 3 lands well delivered together at the end.

Old 401k - What Are Your Options
Nichole Solo 40-45 sec
Gear
  • Mic Mini on Nichole
  • Tripod - single frame
  • Ring light on Nichole
Before you hit record
  • Mic tested
  • Clean professional backdrop
  • Eyes in top third
  • Room quiet
  • Focus locked
Open with this - pause after you say it
"Stop maxing out your 401k until you watch this."
Script
Nichole
When you leave a job most people do one of four things with their old 401k - leave it, roll it, cash it, or forget it entirely. Each one has a completely different tax consequence and most people pick based on whatever's easiest in the moment.
Nichole
Cashing it out triggers income tax plus a 10% penalty if you're under 59 and a half. Leaving it behind means you're not in control of how it's invested. Rolling it into your new employer's plan may limit your options. Rolling it into an IRA gives you the most flexibility and control.
Nichole
The right move depends on your full picture - not just this account.
